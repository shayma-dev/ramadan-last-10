The Last Ten Nights of Ramadan

A structured nightly plan for maximum reward — 9:00 PM to Fajr 4:30 AM

Why These Ten Nights Are Different
«كَانَ النَّبِيُّ ﷺ إِذَا دَخَلَ الْعَشْرُ شَدَّ مِئْزَرَهُ، وَأَحْيَا لَيْلَهُ، وَأَيْقَظَ أَهْلَهُ»
"When the last ten days began, the Prophet ﷺ would tighten his waist-wrapper, stay up the entire night, and awaken his family." — He abandoned all comfort for ten nights without exception.
Sahih Al-Bukhari 2024 · Sahih Muslim 1174
«مَنْ قَامَ لَيْلَةَ الْقَدْرِ إِيمَانًا وَاحْتِسَابًا غُفِرَ لَهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِنْ ذَنْبِهِ»
"Whoever stands in prayer on Laylatul Qadr out of faith and hoping for reward, all his previous sins will be forgiven." — One night wipes a lifetime of sin.
Sahih Al-Bukhari 1901 · Sahih Muslim 760
«مَنْ قَرَأَ فِي لَيْلَةٍ بِأَلْفِ آيَةٍ كُتِبَ مِنَ الْمُقَنْطِرِينَ»
"Whoever recites one thousand verses in a single night will be recorded among Al-Muqantareen — those with treasures beyond measure." — This plan reaches that level every night.
Sunan Abu Dawud 1398 · Ibn Hibban 2572

9:00 PM
📖
Tilawah — Juzz 1 of 3 · ~60 min
Begin the night immediately with the Quran. Every letter earns 10 hasanat. Jibril ﷺ revised the entire Quran with the Prophet ﷺ every Ramadan — opening the night with recitation sets the tone for everything that follows.
  • Recite the first of tonight's three juzz (approx. 20 pages) at a clear, focused pace.
  • Recite with tajweed — slow and precise earns more than fast and careless.
  • If your eyes fill with tears, do not stop. That is one of the highest stations before Allah.
«مَنْ قَرَأَ حَرْفًا مِنْ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ فَلَهُ بِهِ حَسَنَةٌ، وَالْحَسَنَةُ بِعَشْرِ أَمْثَالِهَا»
"Whoever recites one letter of the Book of Allah earns one good deed, and that deed is multiplied ten times."
Sunan At-Tirmidhi 2910 — Graded Sahih

Qiyam — Session 1 of 3 · Type A · جزء تبارك · ~340 Ayat
Night prayer is the most virtuous voluntary worship after the obligatory prayers. Standing before Allah with long surahs is the act that defines the 'Ibad Ar-Rahman — and every ayah in prayer carries a weight beyond ordinary recitation.
  • Pray 4 raka'at in pairs of 2. Make salam after every 2.
  • Keep sujood long — whisper personal dua in it. The closest you are to Allah is in sujood.

Dhikr Station — Al-Baqarah + Dua for Mu'mineen + Salawat
Three acts with extraordinary weight: the last two ayat of Al-Baqarah were gifted from beneath Allah's Throne, the Dua for Mu'mineen earns a hasanah for every believer alive and dead, and Salawat earns ten blessings from Allah for every one you send.

1 · Last Two Ayat of Surah Al-Baqarah (البقرة) — Recite once, with full attention:

آمَنَ الرَّسُولُ بِمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِ وَالْمُؤْمِنُونَ... (البقرة ٢٨٥–٢٨٦)
Recite to the very end of Surah Al-Baqarah — slowly and with reflection.
These verses were sent from beneath the Throne of Allah and "suffice" the one who recites them at night.
«مَنْ قَرَأَ بِالآيَتَيْنِ مِنْ آخِرِ سُورَةِ الْبَقَرَةِ فِي لَيْلَةٍ كَفَتَاهُ»
"Whoever recites the two verses at the end of Surah Al-Baqarah in a night, they will suffice him."
Sahih Al-Bukhari 5009 · Sahih Muslim 807

Mid-Night Energy Break — Family Snack + Quran Discussion
The most demanding part of the night is still ahead — the Last Third begins at 3:00 AM. A short energy break here, with family, re-fuels the body and re-ignites the heart through collective reflection on the Quran you have already recited tonight.
  • Eat a light snack and drink water together. Keep the energy for what is coming, not for what has passed.
  • Discuss the three juzz you will recite tonight — their stories, themes, and what struck you. This is worship, not idle talk.
  • Remind each other: "The last third starts at 3:00 AM. Allah is calling out right now — we will answer."
  • Give Sadaqah now if you haven't already — even a small, sincere amount.
«كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ أَجْوَدَ النَّاسِ، وَكَانَ أَجْوَدُ مَا يَكُونُ فِي رَمَضَانَ»
"The Messenger of Allah ﷺ was the most generous of people, and he was most generous in Ramadan."
Sahih Al-Bukhari 1902 · Sahih Muslim 2308

The Peak — Laylatul Qadr Dua + Personal Duas + Completing All Dhikr
You have completed your Quran target and your prayer target. Allah is descending, calling out, and waiting. This is the moment the entire night was building towards — pour your heart out and do not stop until Fajr.

Suhoor — The Blessed Meal
Suhoor is a sunnah with barakah — it sustains you for the day. You are still inside the Last Third while eating, so do not let this time pass in silence.
  • Eat lightly and drink well. Keep repeating the Laylatul Qadr dua quietly throughout.
  • Continue sending Salawat and making personal duas — you are still in the most sacred time window.
«تَسَحَّرُوا فَإِنَّ فِي السَّحُورِ بَرَكَةً»
"Eat Suhoor, for in Suhoor there is blessing."
Sahih Al-Bukhari 1923 · Sahih Muslim 1095
4:20 AM
🌙
Witr + Final Dua Before Fajr
Witr seals the night. The gap between the Fajr adhan and iqamah is explicitly confirmed as a time when dua is not rejected — use every remaining second.
  • Pray Witr — 1 or 3 raka'at with Dua al-Qunut in the final raka'ah.
  • After Witr: repeat the Laylatul Qadr dua non-stop until the adhan.
  • When the adhan sounds — make dua during it, then again between adhan and iqamah.
«اجْعَلُوا آخِرَ صَلَاتِكُمْ بِاللَّيْلِ وِتْرًا»
"Make Witr the last of your night prayers."
Sahih Muslim 751 · Sahih Al-Bukhari 998
«الدُّعَاءُ بَيْنَ الأَذَانِ وَالإِقَامَةِ لَا يُرَدُّ»
"Dua between the adhan and the iqamah is not rejected."
Sunan Abu Dawud 521 — Graded Sahih · Sunan At-Tirmidhi 212
4:30 AM
🌄
Salat Al-Fajr — The Seal of the Night
Fajr in congregation is equivalent to standing in prayer the entire night. It is the seal of everything you have done — pray it with the same presence as Tahajjud.
«مَنْ صَلَّى الصُّبْحَ فِي جَمَاعَةٍ فَكَأَنَّمَا صَلَّى اللَّيْلَ كُلَّهُ»
"Whoever prays Fajr in congregation is as if he prayed the entire night."
Sahih Muslim 656
